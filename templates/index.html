What are NFTs

NFTs stand for a Non-fungible token. A "Non-fungible” more or less means that it’s unique and can’t be replaced with something else its one of a kind persay.

How do they work?

At a very high level Most NFTs are part of the Ethereum blockchain. Ethereum is a cryptocurrency, like bitcoin or dogecoin, but its blockchain also supports these NFTs, which store extra information that makes them work differently from, say, an ETH coin. It is worth noting that other blockchains can implement their own versions of NFTs as well. NFTs are minted through smart contracts that assign ownership and manage the transferability of the NFT's. When someone creates or mints an NFT, they execute code stored in smart contracts that conform to different standards, such as ERC-721. This information is added to the blockchain where the NFT is being managed.

What can they be?

NFTs can really be anything digital (such as drawings, music, your brain downloaded and turned into an AI), but a lot of the current excitement is around using the tech to sell digital art. Moroever some NFTs have special traits which can allow the NFT to change like gaining skills and evlove and also being able to interact in games that are on the block chain.
